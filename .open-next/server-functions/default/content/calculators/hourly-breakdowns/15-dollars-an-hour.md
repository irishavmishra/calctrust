# $15 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Earning $15 per hour—the target federal minimum wage many advocate for—translates to $31,200 annually working full-time. Here's exactly what $15/hour means for your budget.

## $15/Hour Annual Breakdown

| Period | Gross Amount |
|--------|--------------|
| Hourly | $15.00 |
| Daily (8 hrs) | $120 |
| Weekly (40 hrs) | $600 |
| Biweekly | $1,200 |
| Monthly | $2,600 |
| Annual | $31,200 |

## Take-Home Pay on $15/Hour

| State | Annual Net | Monthly Net |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| No-tax states | ~$27,300 | ~$2,275 |
| Medium-tax | ~$26,500 | ~$2,208 |
| High-tax | ~$25,800 | ~$2,150 |

---

## Is $15/Hour Enough to Live On?

$15/hour provides basic living in affordable areas but remains insufficient for most expensive metros. It's approximately 69% of median individual income.

**Housing budget (30% rule)**: ~$780/month max
