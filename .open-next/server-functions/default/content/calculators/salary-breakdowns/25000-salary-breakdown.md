## Take-Home Pay on $25,000

### Federal Tax

At $25,000, you're in the 12% marginal bracket, but with the standard deduction ($15,000), taxable income is only $10,000.

**Estimated federal tax**: ~$1,000 (4% effective rate)

### FICA Taxes

- Social Security: $1,550
- Medicare: $363
- **Total FICA: $1,913**

### Net Take-Home

| State | Annual Net | Monthly Net |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| No-tax states | ~$22,000 | ~$1,833 |
| Low-tax states | ~$21,500 | ~$1,792 |
| High-tax states | ~$21,000 | ~$1,750 |

## Is $25,000 a Livable Salary?

$25,000 is below the poverty line for some family sizes but can work for single individuals in low-cost areas with careful budgeting. You'll likely qualify for:
- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- Medicaid or ACA subsidies
- SNAP benefits in some cases
