## Deep Dive: Connecticut Tax Structure

### Connecticut's Graduated Tax Rates

Connecticut imposes graduated rates for 2025:

**Single Filers**:
- 2% on income up to $10,000
- 4.5% on income $10,001 to $50,000
- 5.5% on income $50,001 to $100,000
- 6% on income $100,001 to $200,000
- 6.5% on income $200,001 to $250,000
- 6.9% on income $250,001 to $500,000
- 6.99% on income over $500,000

### Connecticut vs. New York

Many workers commute between Connecticut and New York. Key comparisons:

| Scenario | Connecticut | NYC (Resident) |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| $100,000 salary | ~$4,500 state tax | ~$8,500 (state + city) |
| $200,000 salary | ~$10,500 state tax | ~$18,000 (state + city) |

Living in Connecticut while working in NYC can save significant taxes, though commuting costs matter.

### No Local Income Taxes

Connecticut has no local income taxes, simplifying payroll.
