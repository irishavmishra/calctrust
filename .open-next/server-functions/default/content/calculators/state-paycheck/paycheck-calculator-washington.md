## Deep Dive: Washington's Tax-Free Advantage

### Washington Has No Income Tax

Washington has no state income tax on wages, salaries, or other income.

### Washington vs. Oregon

Oregon has income tax up to 9.9%. A $100,000 earner saves ~$7,500 annually living in WA vs. OR.

This creates incentive to live in Washington (Vancouver) while potentially working in Oregon (Portland)—though Oregon taxes income earned there regardless of residency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Washington have state income tax?**
No. Washington has no state income tax on any income.

**How does WA compare to Oregon?**
Oregon has up to 9.9% income tax; WA has 0%. Significant savings for WA residents.

**Does WA have high sales tax?**
Yes. WA's sales tax runs 8-10.5% depending on locality, partially offsetting the income tax advantage.
