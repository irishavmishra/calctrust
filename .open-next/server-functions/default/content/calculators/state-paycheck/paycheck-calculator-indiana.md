## How the Calculation Works

1. **Start with gross pay**: Total earnings before deductions
2. **Subtract pre-tax deductions**: 401(k), health insurance, HSA/FSA
3. **Calculate federal income tax**: Apply IRS brackets
4. **Calculate Indiana state tax**: Apply flat 3.05% rate
5. **Calculate county tax**: Add local county rate (varies 0.5% to 3%+)
6. **Compute FICA**: Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%)
7. **Arrive at net pay**: Your actual take-home

## Deep Dive: Indiana Tax Structure

### Indiana's Low Flat Tax

Indiana's 3.05% flat income tax is among the lowest in the nation for states with income taxes:
- Only Pennsylvania (3.07%) and North Dakota (~2%) come close
- Far below neighboring Illinois (4.95%) and Ohio (variable)

### County Income Taxes

Indiana is unique for extensive county-level income taxes:

| County | Rate | Major City |
|--------|------|------------|
| Marion | 2.02% | Indianapolis |
| Lake | 1.5% | Gary |
| Hamilton | 1.0% | Carmel |
| Allen | 1.35% | Fort Wayne |
| St. Joseph | 1.75% | South Bend |

Combined state + county can reach 5-6% in higher counties.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Indiana's income tax rate?**
Indiana has a flat 3.05% state rate, plus county taxes ranging from about 0.5% to 3%.

**What is my county tax rate?**
Rates vary by county. Marion County (Indianapolis) is 2.02%. Check the Indiana Department of Revenue for your specific county.

**Is Indiana better than Illinois for taxes?**
Yes. Indiana's 3.05% beats Illinois's 4.95% by nearly 2 percentage points, saving significant money.

**Do I have to pay county tax to where I live or work?**
County tax is based on residence county, not work location.
