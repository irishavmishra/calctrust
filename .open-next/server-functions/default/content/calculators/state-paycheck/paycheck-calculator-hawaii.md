## How the Calculation Works

1. **Start with gross pay**: Total earnings before deductions
2. **Subtract pre-tax deductions**: 401(k), health insurance, HSA/FSA
3. **Calculate federal income tax**: Apply IRS brackets
4. **Calculate Hawaii state tax**: Apply graduated rates (1.4% to 11%)
5. **Compute FICA**: Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%)
6. **Arrive at net pay**: Your actual take-home

Hawaii has 12 tax brackets with high top rates:

**Single Filers**:

- Top rate of 11% applies to income over $200,000

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Hawaii's income tax rate?**
Hawaii has graduated rates from 1.4% to 11%, with 11% applying to income over $200,000.

**Is Hawaii's tax higher than California?**
California's top rate (13.3%) is higher, but Hawaii's 11% applies at much lower income levels.

**Is military pay taxed in Hawaii?**
Active-duty military pay is exempt from Hawaii state income tax.
