# $12 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Earning $12 per hour translates to approximately $24,960 annually if you work full-time. This is the federal tipped minimum wage in many states and a common starting wage for entry-level positions. Here's exactly what $12/hour means for your finances.

## $12/Hour Annual Breakdown

| Period | Gross Amount |
|--------|--------------|
| Hourly | $12.00 |
| Daily (8 hrs) | $96 |
| Weekly (40 hrs) | $480 |
| Biweekly | $960 |
| Monthly | $2,080 |
| Annual | $24,960 |

**Calculation**: $12 × 40 hours × 52 weeks = $24,960

---

## Take-Home Pay on $12/Hour

After federal taxes, FICA, and state taxes, your take-home is approximately:

| State | Annual Net | Monthly Net |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| No-tax states | ~$22,200 | ~$1,850 |
| Low-tax states | ~$21,700 | ~$1,808 |
| High-tax states | ~$21,200 | ~$1,767 |

At $12/hour, your tax burden is minimal due to low income brackets and potential EITC eligibility.

---

## Is $12/Hour a Living Wage?

$12/hour is below the living wage in most U.S. cities for single adults without children. It can work in very low-cost areas with frugal budgeting or as a secondary income.

**Affordability check (30% rule)**: $24,960 × 30% = **$624/month max for housing**
